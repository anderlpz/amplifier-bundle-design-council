# Design Council Awareness

When a design decision, mockup, component, or full interface is under review and the
question is broader than any single dimension — "is this actually *good*, and why?" —
convene the Design Intelligence Council with `/design-council <target>`.

The council is worth invoking when:

- A design is "done" but you want orthogonal perspectives before shipping.
- Reviewers keep circling the same surface issues and missing the deeper ones.
- You suspect the work is competent but generic, or polished but hollow.
- You need attributed, specific findings rather than a single vague verdict.

Each lens asks one fundamentally different question, and the tensions between them —
originality vs. coherence, emotion vs. accessibility, craft vs. context — are the
point. The council surfaces those tradeoffs plainly for a human to resolve; it does
not average them away.
